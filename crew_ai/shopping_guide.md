## Complete Preparation Instructions

Based on authentic kheer recipes[1][2]:

**Cooking Time:** 35-40 minutes | **Serves:** 4 people

1. **Toast the rice:** Heat 1 tablespoon ghee (if available) or use milk directly. Add ¼ cup basmati rice and stir for 2-3 minutes until fragrant[1].

2. **Add milk:** Pour 1 liter whole milk into the pan. Bring to a gentle boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally[2].

3. **Simmer:** Lower heat and let simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring frequently. Rice will absorb milk and release starch, creating creaminess[2].

4. **Add sweetener:** When rice is fully cooked, add 4-6 tablespoons sugar and stir well[1].

5. **Add cardamom:** Crush 2-3 green cardamom pods and add powder to kheer. Stir gently[1].

6. **Final consistency:** Add more milk if too thick, or cook longer if too thin. Kheer should have pudding-like texture[1].

7. **Cool and serve:** Allow to cool slightly before serving to 4 people.

## Money-Saving Tips & Strategies

### Immediate Savings (For This Recipe)
1. **Skip saffron entirely** — saves ₹50-100 and kheer remains delicious with cardamom alone[1][2]
2. **Buy cardamom from Indian spice markets** — 40% cheaper than supermarkets (₹40 vs ₹60)
3. **Purchase milk from local dairy** — typically ₹35-40/liter vs ₹45-50 packaged
4. **Use regular white sugar instead of organic** — saves ₹5-10
5. **No nuts needed** — dietary restriction becomes cost benefit; saves ₹30-50

### Long-Term Budget Optimization
1. **Buy rice and sugar in bulk** from wholesale stores or discount retailers. These staples have long shelf lives and are used frequently[2]
2. **Use store loyalty programs** on milk, rice, and sugar purchases for additional discounts
3. **Prepare kheer the day before** to avoid last-minute shopping and ensure milk freshness
4. **Store leftover kheer in refrigerator** for 2-3 days — this recipe yields 4-5 servings, extending value
5. **Share bulk purchases** with neighbors or family to reduce per-person costs

### Quality Over Quantity
1. **Choose aged basmati rice** when possible — superior aroma and texture with no price premium at bulk stores[2]
2. **Full-fat milk is non-negotiable** — low-fat milk produces less creamy consistency; better to reduce portion size than compromise quality
3. **Fresh cardamom pods** from spice markets deliver better flavor than pre-ground powders
